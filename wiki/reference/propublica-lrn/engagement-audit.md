# Engagement Audit — Diagnosing a Callout That Isn't Working

Use when a callout has launched and isn't generating useful responses. Work through these diagnostic areas and return specific fixes.

---

## Problem 1: Too few responses overall

**Diagnosis questions**:
- Where did you post the callout? Was it on your newsroom's website only, or did you take it to where the community is?
- Did you use any trusted intermediaries to distribute it?
- How long has it been live?
- Did you post it once, or did you follow up?

**Common fixes**:
- Repost in new channels, especially Facebook groups, Nextdoor, or WhatsApp networks the community actually uses
- Find a trusted intermediary to share it with their network
- Give it more time — most callout responses come in the first 24 hours after each distribution push, not passively over weeks
- Add a "this callout was shared with me by [trusted person]" line to the post

---

## Problem 2: Responses but not from the right people

**Diagnosis questions**:
- Who is actually responding? What do they have in common?
- Are they the affected community, or advocates, press watchers, and interested observers?
- What channels did you use? (Channels attract their own audiences — a newsroom website attracts news consumers, not necessarily affected residents)

**Common fixes**:
- Move distribution to channels where the target community actually is (not just where journalists are)
- Reframe the ask in language the community uses, not journalism language
- Add specificity to the callout: "We specifically want to hear from parents of children who attended X school between 2015 and 2021" filters out irrelevant responses

---

## Problem 3: Responses but nothing useful in them

**Diagnosis questions**:
- What did you ask? Was it specific enough that you'd know what to do with a yes answer?
- Did your form allow people to tell you what they actually know, or did it only ask the questions you had?
- Is there a freeform text field? (People often put the most useful information in "anything else you want us to know")

**Common fixes**:
- Add or expand a freeform text field
- Revise the ask to be more specific about what you need: not "tell us your experience" but "do you have documents, photos, or test results related to this?"
- Follow up with respondents who seemed promising but gave thin initial responses

---

## Problem 4: No response from the people who matter most

**Diagnosis questions**:
- Are there specific individuals or types of people you most need to hear from?
- What's stopping them from responding? Fear, lack of access, distrust, not seeing the callout?

**Common fixes**:
- Fear of retaliation: Add explicit, specific reassurance about what you will and won't do with their information. "Your name will not appear in the story without your explicit consent" is more reassuring than "confidential."
- Distrust of press: Find an intermediary they trust to vouch for you before asking them to respond
- Not seeing it: You need to reach them directly, not wait for the callout to find them. Use LinkedIn, court records, organizational directories to identify and contact them individually.

---

## Problem 5: Responses that can't be verified or published

**Diagnosis questions**:
- Are you getting anonymous tips without corroboration?
- Are claims specific enough to investigate but not backed by documents?

**Common fixes**:
- Follow up with every promising respondent and ask for the document or the second source
- Use their tip as a lead to public records, not as a standalone claim
- Be honest in the story about what community input you received and how you verified it
