# Verdict Elaboration — Commission, Needs More, Kill

## Commission

The story exists. The central claim is provable with obtainable evidence. Reporting resources can be committed.

A commission verdict must specify what the reporting must establish — not "continue reporting" but "the reporting must confirm X, Y, and Z before this story can publish." This is what the editor will hold the reporter to.

**A commission is not a blank check.** The scope of the commission is defined by what was pitched. A story that grows into different territory during reporting requires a new pitch.

**Condition on Tier 1 results:** Do not commission before running available Tier 1 checks. A commission based on untested Tier 1 hypotheses is a commission on hope, not evidence. If Tier 1 results come back positive, say so in the commission: "Commission, contingent on [specific condition] confirmed by machine research."

---

## Needs More

The story may exist but the evidence base is too thin to commit reporting resources. This is not a soft commission.

A "needs more" verdict must:
1. Name the exact gap: what is missing
2. State the closing condition: what would make it commissionable
3. Specify what is blocked: no source calls, no FOIA filing, no human reporting time — until the gap is closed

**The most common "needs more" trigger:** Tier 1 hypotheses that could be machine-tested before the pitch were not run. Run them. Return with results. Then the pitch can be re-evaluated.

**"Needs more" vs. "route back":** If the evidence base is thin because the theme statement is improperly focused, route back to blundell-theme-statement. "Needs more" assumes the frame is right but the evidence is thin. If the frame is wrong, "needs more" won't fix it.

---

## Kill

The central claim is unprovable as framed, or the evidence does not support it and there is no credible path to evidence that would.

A kill verdict must:
1. State specifically why: what would have to be true for this to be publishable, and what establishes that it isn't
2. State whether a different angle on the same territory is commissionable

The second requirement is important. A kill on one framing is not a kill on the territory. If the story is real but this approach to it isn't, say so: "Kill as framed. The regulatory failure angle requires [X] which doesn't exist. The Congressional action angle — narrower, doesn't depend on proving the regulatory chain — may be commissionable on existing evidence."

---

## The distinction that matters most

**Commission** — this story, as pitched, with specific reporting requirements stated.
**Needs more** — this story, with a specific gap named and a specific closing condition; no reporting until the gap is closed.
**Kill** — not this story as framed; possibly a different story on the same ground.

"Needs more" is the most frequently abused verdict. Its abuse looks like: "I liked the story so I said needs more instead of kill." If the evidence path doesn't exist, it's a kill. If the evidence path exists but isn't yet traveled, it's needs more with a specific closing condition. The distinction is in whether you can state the closing condition — if you can't, it's a kill.
