## Hypothesis Formation

Working from the in-scope chains defined by the fence: the documented pattern of trades across multiple announcements, the asset class and policy domain extension of that pattern, the mandatory surveillance obligations of CME/ICE, whether those systems flagged the trades, whether CFTC received referrals and acted, identifiable harm to counterparties, and the breadth of Congressional response.

---

### Hypothesis 1: The pattern spans multiple White House announcements — not a single event

**IF:** Pre-announcement positioning is systematic rather than coincidental  
**THEN:** Public news archives, financial reporting, and market data would show documented instances of outsized futures or options positioning in oil, equities, or currencies immediately before at least two or three identifiable Trump foreign policy announcements since January 2025  
**AT:** Published WSJ and Reuters reporting; Bloomberg/Reuters market data archives; academic pre-announcement return studies; news archive keyword searches  
**HOW:** Perplexity search for documented trades preceding Trump 2025 foreign policy announcements; cross-reference WSJ's broader pattern documentation against this reporter's independent holdings; xai-grok scan for financial commentary or academic flags on pre-event returns  
**TIER:** 1 — machine  
**CONFIDENCE:** High  
**RATIONALE:** WSJ has already published documentation of a broader pattern; the question is whether this reporter can independently verify at least two to three instances without relying solely on WSJ's reporting — and that determination is in the public record.

---

### Hypothesis 2: The pattern extends beyond oil futures into equities, currencies, or other asset classes

**IF:** Traders with advance knowledge were not constrained to oil futures but positioned across correlated markets  
**THEN:** Public market data would show anomalous options activity or futures positioning in equity indices (particularly defense, energy, or Iran-sensitive sector ETFs), currency pairs, or commodity contracts immediately before relevant announcements  
**AT:** CME and ICE public trade data; options clearinghouse records (OCC public data); financial news archives; academic event-study literature on pre-announcement returns  
**HOW:** Perplexity search for anomalous equity or currency positioning before Trump Iran/tariff announcements 2025; xai-grok scan for financial analysis flagging cross-asset pre-event moves; search OCC public data for options volume spikes  
**TIER:** 1 — machine  
**CONFIDENCE:** Medium  
**RATIONALE:** WSJ documented a broader multi-asset pattern, but whether this reporter can confirm specific asset classes independently — rather than relying on WSJ's characterization — is unresolved; the evidence exists in public data but may require interpretation.

---

### Hypothesis 3: CME and ICE have explicit, publicly documented surveillance obligations that the documented trades would have triggered

**IF:** Exchange surveillance rules define specific thresholds for anomalous pre-event positioning that require internal flagging and potential CFTC referral  
**THEN:** Those thresholds, timelines, and reporting requirements would appear in CME and ICE public rulebooks, CFTC-designated contract market (DCM) core principles (17 CFR Parts 37 and 38), or CFTC surveillance guidance documents  
**AT:** CME Rulebook (cmegroup.com/rulebook); ICE Futures U.S. Rules; CFTC regulations 17 CFR Part 37 (Core Principle 2) and Part 38 (Core Principle 2); CFTC concept releases and staff letters on surveillance  
**HOW:** Perplexity search for CFTC DCM core principle surveillance requirements; direct search of CME and ICE public rulebooks for anomalous trading definitions and referral obligations; search CFTC.gov for published surveillance standards  
**TIER:** 1 — machine  
**CONFIDENCE:** High  
**RATIONALE:** The regulatory framework is entirely public; CFTC core principles for DCMs mandate surveillance programs — the specific thresholds and whether the documented trades would have met them is a document-reading task, not a source task.

---

### Hypothesis 4: CME and ICE surveillance systems generated internal alerts on pre-announcement positioning in the documented trades

**IF:** The documented trades met exchange surveillance thresholds (established by Hypothesis 3)  
**THEN:** Exchange internal compliance records, alert logs, or referral correspondence would document that the trades were flagged  
**AT:** CME and ICE internal compliance records (non-public); CFTC referral acknowledgment database (non-public); exchange compliance officers (sources)  
**HOW:** FOIA to CFTC for referral records from CME and ICE under 17 CFR (note: CFTC is a federal agency and FOIA-able; exchanges as SROs have limited FOIA exposure); source call to current or former exchange surveillance staff; check CFTC Inspector General reports for surveillance gap findings  
**TIER:** 2 — machine + human  
**PRE-COMMISSION MACHINE CHECK:** Hypothesis 3 must be completed first. If the rulebook establishes that trades of this size and timing would have crossed surveillance thresholds, that is sufficient predicate to commission the FOIA and source work here.  
**CONFIDENCE:** High  
**RATIONALE:** Exchanges are required by CFTC core principles to operate surveillance programs; trades of the documented size and timing almost certainly generated alerts — the question is what happened next, and that requires non-public records or sources.

---

### Hypothesis 5: CFTC received exchange referrals on the documented trades and took no public action

**IF:** CME and/or ICE sent referrals to CFTC on anomalous pre-announcement positioning  
**THEN:** CFTC's public enforcement docket would show no investigation, no public statement, and no enforcement action referencing these trades; CFTC chair's Congressional testimony in 2025 would be silent on them; no CFTC press release would reference oil futures pre-announcement positioning  
**AT:** CFTC public enforcement docket (cftc.gov/LawRegulation/Enforcement); CFTC press releases 2025; Congressional testimony transcripts (Senate Agriculture, Senate Banking); CFTC chair public statements  
**HOW:** Perplexity search for CFTC 2025 enforcement actions involving oil futures or pre-announcement trading; direct search of CFTC.gov enforcement docket; search Congressional testimony records for CFTC chair statements on this matter; FOIA to CFTC for referral records from CME/ICE (Tier 2 component)  
**TIER:** 2 — machine + human  
**PRE-COMMISSION MACHINE CHECK (Tier 1 component):** Confirming absence of public CFTC action is machine-testable right now — search the docket, press releases, and testimony. A confirmed absence of public action is itself newsworthy and does not require the FOIA. The referral documentation (whether CFTC *received* referrals before doing nothing) requires human FOIA work. Run the machine check before commissioning the FOIA.  
**CONFIDENCE:** Medium  
**RATIONALE:** Confirming CFTC non-action is straightforward; confirming that referrals were *received* before non-action occurred — the distinction between two different failure modes flagged as a dealbreaker in the theme statement gap list — requires non-public records and lowers the confidence on the full hypothesis.

---

### Hypothesis 6: Institutional investors (pension funds, passive allocators) are identifiable as counterparties who absorbed losses on the documented trades

**IF:** Uninformed institutional investors were on the losing side of these trades  
**THEN:** CFTC large trader reports, CME clearing records, or SEC 13F filings would reveal offsetting positions held by institutional names around the relevant announcement dates  
**AT:** CFTC large trader reporting data (partially public via Commitments of Traders reports); CME clearing records (non-public); SEC EDGAR 13F filings; DTCC clearing records (non-public)  
**HOW:** Search CFTC Commitments of Traders (COT) reports for commercial/non-commercial positioning in oil futures around announcement dates; search SEC EDGAR for 13F filings showing large institutional oil futures exposure at relevant dates; FOIA to CFTC for large trader data; source calls to institutional trading desks  
**TIER:** 2 — machine + human  
**PRE-COMMISSION MACHINE CHECK (Tier 1 component):** COT reports are public and machine-searchable — they will show aggregate positioning by trader category (commercial, non-commercial, swap dealer) around the announcement dates, establishing whether institutional-category traders held the offsetting positions at scale.  
**CONFIDENCE:** Low  
**RATIONALE:** The mathematical existence of counterparties is certain, but identifying specific named institutions requires non-public clearing data; even with FOIA, clearing records may not yield named counterparties, making the "identifiable harm" claim fragile.

> **FLAG — judgment call on confidence:** This chain is logically certain at the aggregate level (counterparties always exist) but low-confidence at the level of named-institution identification, which is what the theme statement gap list calls out as a dealbreaker for moral weight. The confidence level here reflects the *named* claim, not the existence claim. If the unnamed aggregate harm is sufficient for the story, confidence rises to medium.

---

### Hypothesis 7: Congressional attention is building beyond Murphy — committee chairs are moving toward formal inquiry

**IF:** Institutional momentum toward Congressional oversight is growing  
**THEN:** Public statements, press releases, committee letters, or news coverage would document additional members or committee chairs (Senate Agriculture, Senate Banking, House Financial Services) signaling intent to investigate  
**AT:** Congressional Record; committee press releases (Senate Agriculture Committee, Senate Banking Committee, House Financial Services Committee); members' official .gov press pages; news archive  
**HOW:** Perplexity search for Congress + Trump trades + investigation + committee 2025; search committee press release pages directly; xai-grok scan for recent Congressional statements on market manipulation and White House trades  
**TIER:** 1 — machine  
**CONFIDENCE:** Medium  
**RATIONALE:** Murphy's statement is in the public record; whether others have moved is a factual question answerable by public statements — but Congressional attention can be privately cultivated without public signaling, so machine search may undercount actual activity.
