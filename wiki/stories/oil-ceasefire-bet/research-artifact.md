## Confirmed Claims (with sources)

### The Trade
- At 19:45 GMT on April 7, 2026, someone sold 8,600 lots of crude futures — 6,200 Brent on ICE, 2,400 WTI on NYMEX — with a notional value of ~$950M. Confirmed by Reuters/LSEG data.
- Execution occurred during an unusually low-volume window. Described by Reuters/LSEG as "entirely atypical" for that scale at that hour.
- Oil fell ~15% the following day after Trump's announcement.
- Source: Barchart, OE Digital, AInvest (all 2026-04-09)

### The Political Response
- Rep. Ritchie Torres (D-NY) sent a letter to the SEC and CFTC on April 8, 2026 calling for a formal investigation.
- Torres described the timing as "statistically impossible" without advance knowledge and called it potentially "one of the largest instances of insider trading in history."
- Neither the SEC nor CFTC has responded publicly as of April 9, 2026.
- Source: Multiple outlets citing Torres letter, 2026-04-09

### Jurisdiction
- The CFTC has primary jurisdiction over oil futures under the Commodity Exchange Act.
- The SEC may coordinate on equity futures components of the trade.
- Both agencies were addressed in Torres's letter due to reported spikes in both oil and equity futures.

### The Ceasefire Timeline
- March 21: Trump issued 48-hour ultimatum to Iran via Truth Social.
- March 23: Trump announced "productive talks," delayed strikes 5 days. Iran denied negotiations.
- March 26: Deadline extended 10 days to April 6 at Iran's request.
- April 1: Trump claimed Iran requested ceasefire. Iran denied.
- April 4-5: Trump issued 48-hour final warnings with explicit targets (power plants, oil wells, Kharg Island, desalination plants).
- April 7-8: Ceasefire finalized, mediated by Pakistan in Islamabad. Pakistan PM Shehbaz Sharif announced it publicly. Iran submitted a 10-point plan via Pakistan.
- Internal approval: Supreme Leader Khamenei approved at the last minute, nudged by China.
- Pre-announcement knowledge confirmed: Pakistan, China, Trump, Khamenei. All others unconfirmed.
- Source: Multiple outlets, 2026-04-09

### The Suspect Pool
- The ceasefire was mediated by Pakistan. Only Pakistan, China, Trump, and Khamenei are confirmed to have known before the public announcement.
- US-Iran contacts described as "indirect channels" — no direct US-Iran talks confirmed.
- The trade was executed at 19:45 GMT on April 7 — approximately 2.5 hours before the public announcement.
- If only four parties knew, any US-side leak traces back to Trump's immediate circle or US intelligence with access to the Pakistan channel.

## Open Questions (What Needs Reporting)

1. **Who placed the trade?** CFTC has the records. Trader identity requires: a regulatory source, a broker source, or a successful FOIA/congressional inquiry. Torres's letter may compel disclosure.
2. **Is there a March precedent?** Some reporting mentions a prior trade before Trump's March 23 "productive talks" announcement. Not independently confirmed at same specificity. Needs verification.
3. **Are the Polymarket wallets connected?** 4 new wallets profited ~$600K on ceasefire prediction markets. Same information source as the futures trader? Requires blockchain analysis.
4. **Has Torres received any informal response?** Official silence doesn't mean no contact. A Torres spokesperson call is the fastest move.
5. **Who in the US chain had access to Pakistan channel communications?** State Department, NSC, CIA. Standard diplomatic intel flow. A diplomatic beat reporter can map this.
