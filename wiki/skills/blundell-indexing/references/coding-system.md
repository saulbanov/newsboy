# Coding System

Blundell's shorthand for logging material into the index. The goal is to create entries short enough to scan quickly but specific enough to find the right source and content type without returning to the original.

---

## Source codes

**T** = Interview (from "talk")
**D** = Document

Each source gets a sequential number when you first log it: T1, T2, T3... D1, D2, D3...

The number stays with that source throughout the index. If T5 appears under History, Scope, and Countermoves, all three entries reference T5.

---

## Content type codes

**q** = Direct quote worth using
**O.** = Observation (factual statement, not a quote)
**nf** = National figures (statistics or data of broad scope)
**lf** = Local figures (statistics or data specific to a location)
**Ill.** = Illustration from life (a scene, anecdote, or specific example)
**w/** = With (used to specify what the content is about)

These can be combined: `T3-q, rrc fines` means interview 3 contains a usable quote about RRC fines.

---

## Log format

Each entry: `[Source code]-[Content type], [3-5 word reminder of content]`

Examples:
- `T5-q, druthers, dry up` — interview 5 has a quote about cities wanting to dry up reservations
- `T5-nf ind wtr gao` — interview 5 has national figures on Indian water issues from a GAO report
- `T5-Ill. kokomo w/litig Bmsvl` — interview 5 has an illustration of the Kokomo tribe using litigation against Boomsville
- `D2-O. permit dates 2019-22` — document 2 contains an observation about permit dates from 2019-22
- `T3-q, felt betrayed` — interview 3 has a quote about feeling betrayed

The reminder only needs to be specific enough that you can identify the material when you return to the index. Abbreviate freely.

---

## Multi-use entries

If a piece of material serves more than one section, log it in both places. Don't try to choose. You'll decide which section it belongs to when you write.

Example: A source describes both the scope of contamination (how many sites) and the reason it happened (regulatory capture). Log it under both Scope and Reasons.

---

## What the codes tell you at a glance

When you've finished indexing, scan each section. The mix of content types tells you the quality of your reporting:

- Heavy on **q** (quotes) with no **Ill.** → you have opinion but no scenes. Thin.
- Heavy on **O.** with no **q** → factual but voiceless. Needs human material.
- Heavy on **nf/lf** → data-rich but may be abstract. Needs illustration.
- **Ill.** entries are the gold. These are the scenes that make the story concrete.
- Missing content types in an important section → gap. Note it.
