# Worked Example — Indian Water Rights Interview

This is Blundell's own example of indexing a single productive interview across multiple categories. The interview is the fifth logged, so it carries code T5.

---

## The interview

The source is the attorney for a tribe (called the Kokomo in the example). In one interview he covered:

1. A lawsuit the tribe won against the nearby city of Boomsville for wrongfully using Indian water
2. A quote about the scope of the problem: "If the cities had their druthers, they'd dry up every reservation in the country"
3. National figures from a GAO report about water conflicts involving Indians
4. Historical context: 20 years ago the city was only a town, the tribe had little irrigated agriculture and relied on sheep. Plenty of water for both. Now the grass is gone, the Indians want to irrigate 50,000 acres, and the city has become a metropolis.
5. The reason for conflict: both parties competing for growing shares of a limited supply

---

## How it gets logged

**Under History:**
`T5—O. Kokomo econ 20, sheep.`
An observation on the tribal economy 20 years ago, based on sheep raising.

**Under Scope:**
`T5-q, druthers, dry up—nf ind wtr gao.`
Two entries combined. First: a usable quote about the scope of the problem (the "druthers" line). Second: national figures on Indian water issues from the GAO.

**Under Countermoves:**
`T5-Ill. kokomo w/litig Bmsvl.`
An illustration of Indians fighting back — a lawsuit pressed and won against Boomsville.

**Under Reasons:**
`T5-O. Kokomo-Bmsvl comptn: grwth v. ltd. sply.`
An observation on the reason for conflict — competing growth needs against a limited supply.

---

## What this demonstrates

One interview yields four entries under four different categories. The interview is not logged once under "T5 — tribal attorney." It is broken apart and each fragment placed where it belongs in the story structure.

This is organizing by story aspect, not by source. When you write the History section, you find T5 there. When you write Scope, you find T5 there too. You don't have to remember "I think the attorney said something about history in that long interview."

The Countermoves entry is an Illustration — a scene of action (a lawsuit actually filed and won). Not a quote about fighting back. Not an observer saying the tribe is determined. An action with an outcome. This is what countermoves reporting looks like when it's done right.

---

## Applying the system to your own material

After logging each interview and document this way, your index shows:
- Which categories have many entries (the story's weight is here)
- Which categories have few entries (either thin reporting or sections that don't belong)
- What kinds of material you have: quotes, observations, figures, illustrations
- Exactly where to find each fragment when you write

The index replaces your memory. You write off the index. You return to original sources only to verify exact wording.
