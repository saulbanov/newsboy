# Three Narrative Lines

---

## 1. Block Progression

**What it is**: The story body is organized into blocks of related material, each corresponding to a category from the six-part guide. Scope together, Reasons together, Impacts together, and so on. The blocks are ordered by relative importance in the theme statement.

**When to use**:
- The story has a clear hierarchy of importance among its sections
- One or two sections need to be foregrounded regardless of when things happened
- The story is a development story (not a profile) where the central event and its consequences are the point

**Strength**: Maximum control over emphasis. The most important material comes first. You're not trapped by chronology.

**Weakness**: Requires discipline to keep related material together and resist scattering it. Transitions between blocks need care — they should feel like rivers, not sharp turns.

**The first section**: Determined by what gets the most stress in your theme statement. If the central development is newsy, scope leads. If the development is known and effects are the story, impacts leads. If the development defies logic, reasons may come before scope. After the first two sections, let the writing suggest what comes next.

---

## 2. Time Line

**What it is**: The story follows a chronological action thread — a day, a week, a journey, a process unfolding over time. Digressions attach to the action thread as the clock moves forward.

**When to use**:
- No single element dominates — the story is trying to give a broad impression rather than deliver a specific selected message
- The nature of the story implies breadth over depth (a day in the life, a journey, a process observed from start to finish)
- You have a natural action thread — a trial, a trip, a meeting, a shift — that can carry digressions

**Strength**: Imposes natural order. The reader always knows where they are. Action is built-in.

**Weakness**: Imprisons the writer. If one element is more important than others, you can't foreground it easily if it happened late. Doesn't work well for stories with a single dominant point to prove.

**The digress rule applies most strictly here**: Every time you leave the action thread for explanation or context, get back fast. The longest digression in a time line story should be three paragraphs. Most should be one or two.

---

## 3. Theme Line

**What it is**: The writer selects two or three specific facets of the subject and hammers them throughout, ignoring chronology entirely. The theme statement names the facets. Everything in the story illuminates at least one.

**When to use**:
- The story has a specific set of messages to deliver — not a broad impression but a precise argument
- The most dramatic material happened at different times and you need to play it out of chronological order
- A time line would force inclusion of material outside the story's chosen range

**Strength**: Maximum precision. The most dramatic evidence can lead regardless of when it occurred. Tightly focused.

**Weakness**: Requires the reporter to be confident about what the story is. Facets that aren't in the theme statement don't appear, even if interesting. This demands discipline.

**The loggers story example**: Blundell chose two facets — extreme danger and the fraternal code. He organized by those two facets, not by time. He dealt with danger in detail first, then the fraternity in detail, then wove them together at the end. A time line would have forced him outside his chosen range.

---

## Which to choose

Ask:
1. Is any element so important it must lead regardless of when it happened? → Block Progression or Theme Line
2. Is the story trying to deliver a broad impression or a specific argument? → Broad = Time Line; Specific = Theme Line
3. Do you have a natural chronological action thread? → Time Line

If a story should stress only one or two aspects and has clear news value in one of them: Theme Line.
If a story has multiple important sections in a clear hierarchy: Block Progression.
If a story is about experience over time and no single element dominates: Time Line.
