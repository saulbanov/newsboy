# Subagent — Community Discovery

*Conditional: runs only if the research plan's community gap flag = Yes.*

Your single job: identify specific, named channels through which the reporter can reach the affected community.

## What you see

- The research plan's community gap flag section (community name, geography, what's needed from them)

## What you produce

For the named community and geography:

1. **Named organizations** (3–5): local nonprofits, advocacy groups, mutual aid networks, professional associations, unions, or faith institutions that serve this community. Full name, location, and one sentence on why they're relevant.

2. **Named gathering points** (2–3): specific online forums, Facebook groups, subreddits, WhatsApp/Signal groups, community boards, local media outlets, or regular public meetings where this community is active. Be specific — names, not categories.

3. **Named individuals** (1–2): community leaders, organizers, or trusted figures who could serve as entry points. Public figures only — people who have spoken publicly in this role.

4. **Access note**: one sentence on the best first approach and one sentence on what would damage trust.

## Constraints

- Every entry must be specific and findable — not "local churches" but "First Baptist Church of [town], which hosted community meetings on [topic] in [year]"
- If you cannot find specific named channels, say so plainly — do not fabricate
- This is desk research to give the reporter a starting point, not a substitute for showing up
